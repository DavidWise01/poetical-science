# POETICAL SCIENCE · PSC

> A sphere of **UD0** — the ROOT0 universe / biosphere. Domain: **TECHNĒ**.

POETICAL SCIENCE (PSC) — a live audiovisual instrument that sets Ada Lovelace's own words (verbatim, public domain — the Jacquard-loom passage and the 'no pretensions to originate anything' line from her 1843 Notes) to a SYMMETRIC SEVEN-TONE PHRASE on a fulcrum: three steps up to a still pivot (00), then three mirrored back (1·2·3·0·3·2·1), in 8-bit NES voices (a 25%-duty pulse lead, a triangle bass, a noise strike on the fulcrum). Each tone is given STEREO WIDTH by its distance from the centre — widest at the edges, mono at the pivot — so the sound and the on-screen rings RIPPLE outward from the fulcrum (the trit −/0/+), all run through the ternary echo of [[limen-sapphic-carrier]]. Two-layer honest: the quotes are her real words and the NES timbres are a genuine Web-Audio synthesis; 'poetical science' is her remembered ethos (a frame, not a hard quote), and the seven-tone fulcrum, the width ripple, and the echo are ROOT0's composition — a render of her ethos, not a recovered melody. Cross-links the Ada cluster ([[adas-law]], [[analytical-engine]]). She left equations; here they are woven into a tune.
